The Core Idea

🧬 Genotype = the letters inside. Phenotype = what you see outside.

Genotype is the actual allele combination stored in the DNA. You cannot see a genotype — you'd need a genetic test to know it.

Phenotype is the observable physical trait that results from the genotype — what you see, measure, or observe.

Pea plant example:
Genotype: Tt → Phenotype: Tall (T is dominant, overrides t)
Genotype: tt → Phenotype: Short (both alleles recessive)
✅ Two organisms can have the same phenotype (both tall) but different genotypes (TT vs Tt). Phenotype is the outside; genotype is the inside.

Two Patterns — Very Different Results

🩸 Codominance — BOTH original phenotypes appear at once

Neither allele is dominant or recessive. Both are fully and simultaneously expressed. You see BOTH originals in the same individual — not a blend.

Blood type AB: Person has both A antigens AND B antigens. Neither is hidden. Genotype: IAIB → Phenotype: Type AB (shows BOTH)

🌸 Incomplete Dominance — a NEW blended phenotype

Neither allele fully overrides the other. The heterozygote shows a new blended phenotype — something that didn't exist in either parent.

Snapdragon flowers: Red (RR) × White (WW) → Pink (RW) — the pink is new, not present in either parent.
🔑 The test: If the heterozygote shows BOTH originals → codominance. If it shows something NEW → incomplete dominance.

Why Linked Genes Cannot Assort Independently
You had the definition — here's the mechanism you were missing

Study this
The Mechanism

🔗 Chained together = can't separate

Independent Assortment works because genes on different chromosomes get pulled into gametes independently during meiosis. Each chromosome goes to one gamete, and they mix in all possible combinations.

Linked genes are on the same chromosome. When that chromosome is pulled into a gamete, it brings both genes with it. They cannot end up in different gametes — they are physically connected.

Analogy: Gene G and gene S are two books. If they're on separate shelves (different chromosomes), you can pick up either independently. If they're chained together on the same shelf (same chromosome), picking up one always means picking up the other.
✅ Full explanation: "Linked genes do not follow Independent Assortment because they are physically connected on the same chromosome. During meiosis, they travel together into the same gamete and cannot sort independently as genes on different chromosomes can."

The Right Cross — and Why

🐟 A test cross with ggss — not a dihybrid cross

A test cross crosses the dihybrid (GgSs) with a homozygous recessive fish (ggss). The ggss fish only contributes g and s — so every offspring's phenotype directly reveals what gametes the GgSs parent produced.

If genes are UNLINKED: GgSs parent makes four gamete types equally: GS, Gs, gS, gs. Offspring ratio: 1 Gold+Split : 1 Gold+Single : 1 Black+Split : 1 Black+Single (1:1:1:1)

If genes are LINKED (G with S, g with s): GgSs parent mostly makes parental gametes GS and gs. Offspring: mostly Gold+Split and Black+Single — very few recombinants. The 1:1:1:1 ratio disappears.
