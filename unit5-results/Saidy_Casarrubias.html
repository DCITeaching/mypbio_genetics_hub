Genotype vs Phenotype
Genotype and phenotype are not the same as dominant and recessive
2 marks to recover
Study this
The Core Idea

🧬 Genotype = the letters inside. Phenotype = what you see outside.

Genotype is the actual allele combination stored in the DNA — e.g. Tt, BB, rr. You can't see it without a genetic test.

Phenotype is the observable physical trait that results — e.g. tall plant, brown eyes, Type A blood.

Pea plant: Genotype Tt → Phenotype: tall (T is dominant).
Genotype tt → Phenotype: short (both alleles recessive).
✅ Two organisms can share the same phenotype (both tall) but have different genotypes (TT vs Tt). Phenotype = outside; genotype = inside.

Punnett Ratios & Offspring Calculations
Complete all 4 boxes, then use the fraction ¼
3 marks to recover
Study this
Skill 1 — Reading the genotypic ratio

📊 Count what's in the boxes — in order

From Tt × Tt: boxes are TT, Tt, Tt, tt.

TT: 1 time → 1
Tt: 2 times → 2
tt: 1 time → 1

Genotypic ratio = 1 TT : 2 Tt : 1 tt
⚠️ The ratio goes in order: homozygous dominant : heterozygous : homozygous recessive. Many students write 2:1:1 or 3:1 — check the order carefully.
Skill 2 — Calculating offspring numbers

🧮 Multiply probability × total

tt = ¼ of offspring. Total = 800.

800 × ¼ = 800 ÷ 4 = 200 offspring

Multiply by the fraction (¼), not just the numerator (1). "800 × 1 = 800" is a common error.

✅ Quick check: 200 out of 800 = 25%. That matches the ¼ probability. Always verify your answer matches your stated probability.

Why Daughters Cannot Have the Condition

2 marks to recover
Study this
Why daughters cannot have the condition

🧬 The father's X chromosome is the key

The father in this cross is XAY — he has one X chromosome and one Y. His X chromosome carries the dominant normal allele (XA).

Every daughter he has must inherit his X chromosome (because daughters are XX, meaning one X from dad, one X from mum). Since dad's X is XA, every daughter gets at least one XA.

Even if mum passes Xa (the affected allele), the daughter has XAXa — she is a carrier, not affected. The XA from dad dominates.

Probability that a daughter is affected = 0%
✅ The answer is 0%, not 25% or 50%. Daughters always get XA from this father — they can be carriers but never affected.

1 mark to recover
Study this
Two Patterns — Very Different Results

🩸 Codominance — BOTH original phenotypes appear at once

Neither allele dominates. Both are fully and simultaneously expressed. You see BOTH original phenotypes — not a blend.

Blood type AB: Person has both A antigens AND B antigens. Neither is hidden. IAIB → Type AB (shows BOTH)

🌸 Incomplete Dominance — a NEW blended phenotype

Neither allele fully overrides the other. The heterozygote shows a new blended phenotype not present in either parent.

Snapdragons: Red (RR) × White (WW) → Pink (RW) — the pink is new.
🔑 The test: BOTH originals shown → codominance. NEW blended phenotype → incomplete dominance.

Study this
Three Rules — Work Every Time

📋 Rule 1 — Affected = homozygous recessive

If a person has an autosomal recessive condition, they MUST have two recessive alleles.

Individual [1] is filled (affected) → genotype = ff

📋 Rule 2 — Unaffected with affected child/sibling = carrier

If an unaffected person has an affected child or sibling, they must carry one recessive allele.

Individual [3] is unaffected but has affected child [1] → genotype = Ff

📋 Rule 3 — Always write TWO letters

Every organism has two alleles. A single letter (F or f alone) is never a valid genotype.

✅ Always: FF, Ff, or ff. Never just F or f.

Linked Genes — Definition First
Start with where they are, then explain why it matters
2 marks to recover
Study this
The Mechanism

🔗 Chained together = can't separate

Independent Assortment works because genes on different chromosomes are pulled into gametes independently during meiosis — they mix in all combinations.

Linked genes are on the same chromosome. When that chromosome moves into a gamete, it brings both genes with it. They physically cannot end up in different gametes.

Analogy: Two books chained on the same shelf — picking one always means picking the other.
✅ Full explanation: "Linked genes do not follow Independent Assortment because they are physically connected on the same chromosome and travel together into the same gamete during meiosis, so they cannot sort independently."

Study this
The Right Cross — and Why

🐟 A test cross with ggss — not a general dihybrid cross

Cross the dihybrid (GgSs) with a homozygous recessive fish (ggss). ggss only contributes g and s — so offspring phenotypes directly reveal what gametes GgSs produced.

If UNLINKED: GgSs produces GS, Gs, gS, gs equally.
Offspring ratio: 1 Gold+Split : 1 Gold+Single : 1 Black+Split : 1 Black+Single (1:1:1:1)

If LINKED (G with S, g with s): GgSs mostly produces parental gametes GS and gs.
Offspring: mostly Gold+Split and Black+Single. The 1:1:1:1 ratio disappears.
✅ A complete 3-mark answer needs all three: (1) specific cross GgSs × ggss, (2) expected result if unlinked (1:1:1:1), and (3) expected result if linked (parental types dominate).
